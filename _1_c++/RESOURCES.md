## BOOKS (Read in Order)

| # | Book | Author | Why |
|---|------|--------|-----|
| 1 | *A Tour of C++* (3rd ed.) | Bjarne Stroustrup | Quick overview from the creator, covers C++20 |

| 5 | *C++ Move Semantics: The Complete Guide* | Nicolai Josuttis | Master the hardest part of modern C++ |
| 6 | *C++ Templates: The Complete Guide* (2nd ed.) | Vandevoorde, Josuttis, Gregor | Definitive template reference |

## STUDY STRATEGY

1. **Read → Code → Break → Fix**: For every concept, write code, then deliberately break it to understand error messages.
2. **Read the error messages**: C++ errors are verbose but informative. Learn to parse them.
3. **Use Compiler Explorer**: Always check what your code compiles to.
4. **One new thing per day**: Even 30 minutes of focused study compounds.
5. **Build projects early**: Don't just do exercises — build things you care about.
6. **Read other people's code**: Study open-source C++ projects (folly, abseil, LLVM).
